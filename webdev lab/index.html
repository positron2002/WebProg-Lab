Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in our solar system. It has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Venus is known as Earthâ€™s twin because of its similar size and composition. It has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a balanced atmosphere, liquid water, and a magnetic field that shields it from harmful solar radiation.

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, is known for its dusty red surface and the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons. Scientists are exploring its potential for life.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It has a strong magnetic field and is famous for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has lasted for centuries.

Saturn

Saturn is well known for its beautiful ring system, made of ice and rock particles. It has many moons, including Titan, which has a dense atmosphere.

Uranus

Uranus is a gas giant with a bluish color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, making its axis unique among the planets.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has a deep blue color. It has strong winds and storms, including the Great Dark Spot.
